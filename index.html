Android is a Linux-based mobile operating system (OS) developed by the Open Handset Alliance, which is led by Google. Android is one of the best-selling smartphone platforms worldwide, with over 700,000 Android devices activated every day as of December 2011.

It was initially developed by a small company aptly named Android. It was purchased in 2005 by Google Inc. It is used by a wide variety of manufacturers like HTC, Motorola, LG, Samsung, and many others. Most of the manufacturers just happen to be apart of the Open Handset Alliance as well.

Android OS version software releases have a unique characteristic of being named after various deserts. 1.5 was known as doughnut, 1.6 was referred to as cupcake, etc. The list of Android OS versions are as follows:

  • 1.5: Doughnut
  • 1.6: Cupcake
  • 2.0: Eclair
  • 2.1: Eclair
  • 2.2: Froyo
  • 2.3: Gingerbread
  • 3.0: Honeycomb (tablet only)
  • 4.0: Ice Cream Sandwich (table and mobile OS)

In regards to the design of the Android UI (user interface) it is very similar to another very popular mobile OS, with that OS being the iOS of Apple's iPhone line. It is because of this fact that Android is viewed as user-friendly like the famous OS from Apple. However, there are enough differences between the two warrant Android as something other than an iOS clone.
